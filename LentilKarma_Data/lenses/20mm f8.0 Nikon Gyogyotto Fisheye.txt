lens info = A quirky piece of Nikon history, the Gyogyotto 20 (Fisheye Type 20mm f/8) was not a regular production lens but part of the 1995–2003 “Nikon Amusing/Fun Fun LensSet” sold only in Japan. It was designed less as a serious optic and more as a playful, affordable way for photographers to experiment with extreme wide angles and distortion hence the whimsical name “Gyogyotto”. Rather than aiming for cutting‑edge performance, the lens embraced its limitations: it had a fixed f/8 aperture and fixed focus, with no focus or aperture rings, making it almost a “toy” lens for creative fisheye effects. Its diagonal field of view (~153°) delivers dramatic distortion and exaggerated perspective that photographers either love or struggle to control. The Gyogyotto 20 reflects Nikon’s willingness in the 1990s to release novelty optics alongside its professional lineup, giving shooters a fun, distinctive tool that’s now a collectible curiosity in the world of Nikon glass.
